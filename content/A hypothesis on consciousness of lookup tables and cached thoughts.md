A [[Functionalism|functionalist]]/[[Computationalism|computationalist]] hypothesis – an analogy between lookup tables and cached thoughts.

Let there be a giant lookup table (GLUT) that is functionally equivalent to a biological brain.

Assuming [[Computationalism|computationalism]], The GLUT's subjective experiences come from initially computing the GLUT and, from then on, only from mapping inputs to outputs (retrieval). At the moment of retrieval, less is experienced than in the case of the brain because most of what the brain computes (and re-computes) is pre-computed in the GLUT.

Similarly to the GLUT, the brain has some computations cached, and their use is mostly unconscious.
