We start from the assumption that [[Phenomenal consciousness|phenomenal consciousness]] (subjective experiences) is the only thing that matters.

## Case 1: phenomenal consciousness is real

This implies that what matters are subjective experiences, which are real things.

Some worlds are better than others.

## Case 2: there is no phenomenal consciousness

This implies metaphysical materialism and axiological nihilism. All is "matter" and nothing matters.

The world can be complex, there can be diverse life, or just deserts – it doesn't matter.

## Case 3: phenomenal consciousness is real and the external world is simulated

We assume a world where all subjective experiences are like in our universe, but the external physical world is replaced with a simulated world.

What happens outside of the simulation doesn't matter.
