## Limit of a real Function

Let $f$ be a [[Real function|real function]].

**$f(x)$ tends to the limit $L$ as $x$ tends to $c$** iff
$$
\begin{align}
	\forall & \epsilon\in\R_{>0} : \exists \delta\in\R_{>0} : \forall x\in \dom(f) \setminus \{c\} : \\
	&\left|x-c\right| < \delta \implies \left| f(x) - L\right| < \epsilon .
\end{align}
$$

## Notation

The statement that **$f(x)$ tends to the limit $L$ as $x$ tends to $c$** is denoted with $$\lim_{x\to c} f(x) = L \text.$$
