Let $X$ and $Y$ be sets.  
Let $f\colon X\to Y$ and $g\colon X\to X$.  
$f$ is **invariant to** $g$ iff 
$$
f \circ g = f. 
$$

## Equivalent statement

$f$ is **invariant to** $g$ iff
$$
\forall x\in X\colon f(g(x)) = f(x) .
$$
## To a set of functions

Let $X$ and $Y$ be sets.  
Let $f\colon X\to Y$ and $G\subseteq X\to X$.  
$f$ is **invariant to** a set of functions $G$ iff
	$f$ is invariant to each $g\in G$.
