Let $f\colon X \to Y$ be a [[Function|function]].

$f$ is a **bijection** between sets $X$ and $Y$ iff there exists a [[Function|function]] $f^{-1} \colon Y\to X$ such that
$$
f^{-1}\circ f  = \operatorname{id} .
$$

## Notes

By the definition of a [[Function|function]], if $f$ is bijective between $X$ and $Y$, it is not bijective between $X$ and any $Y'\supset Y$.
