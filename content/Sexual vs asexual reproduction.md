In [Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Information-Theory%2C-Inference%2C-and-Learning-MacKay/f7f15848cd0fbb3d08f351595da833b1627de9c3) (chapter 19), MacKay defines a simple model where there are $G$ genes, each gene is either good or bad, fitness is the proportion of good genes, and mutations are equally likely to flip any gene. The model implies the following:
> A population that reproduces by recombination can acquire information from natural selection at a rate of order $\sqrt G$ times faster than a parthenogenetic population, and it can tolerate a mutation rate that is of order $\sqrt G$ times greater.

Other notes:
> However, since recombination gives the biggest advantage to species whose fitness functions are additive, we might predict that evolution will have favoured species that used a representation of the genome that corresponds to a fitness function that has only weak interactions.
