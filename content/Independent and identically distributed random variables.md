Let $X=\{\rvar x_1, ..,\rvar x_n\}$ be a [[set]] of [[Random variable|random variables]] on a [[Probability space|probability space]] $(\Omega,\Sigma,P)$.

The random variables in $X$ are:
- **independent** iff$$  
\forall \{\rvar z_1, .., \rvar z_m\} \subseteq X:  
\P(\rvar z_1\in A_1, .., \rvar z_m\in A_m) = \prod_{i\in\{1\bidot m\}} \P(\rvar z_i\in A_i) \text,
$$
- **identically distributed** iff $$
\forall i,j\in\{1\bidot n\}: \operatorname\Pi[\rvar x_i] = \operatorname\Pi[\rvar x_j] \text.
$$

## Used notation

- $\operatorname\Pi[\rvar x_i]$ is the [[Distribution of a random variable|distribution of]] $\rvar x_i$.
