[[Notation]]

Let $\rvar x_1, .., \rvar x_n$ be [[Random variable|random variables]] on a [[Probability space|probability space]] $(\Omega,\Sigma,P)$, and let $R$ be a [[predicate]]. It is usual to use an [[expression]] of the form $R(\rvar x_1, .., \rvar x_n)$ to denote an [[Event (probability theory)|event]] where $\rvar x_i$ take values that satisfy the predicate $R$. The corresponding event is $\{\omega\in\Omega\colon R(\rvar x_1(\omega), .., \rvar x_n(\omega))\}$.

For probability, a usual notation is $\P(R(\rvar x_1, .., \rvar x_n))$. An example of such notation is $\P(\rvar x_1 > \rvar x_2, \rvar x_3 \in A)$, where comma denotes conjunction. It is usually assumed that the probability space has an event that corresponds to any set of values of random variables that is of interest.
