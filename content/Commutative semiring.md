A **commutative semiring** is an [[Algebraic structure|algebraic structure]] $(K, +, \cdot)$ that satisfies the following:
- $(K, +)$ is a [[Commutative monoid|commutative monoid]].
- $(K, \cdot)$ is a [[Commutative monoid|commutative monoid]].
- The **distributive law** holds: $$  
	\forall a,b,c \in K : (a\cdot b) + (a\cdot c) = a\cdot (b + c) \text.$$

## Notes

- The identity elements of $+$ and $\cdot$ are often denoted $0$ and $1$.
